Mackinac Island is a picturesque destination. A small island community situated in Michigan with a population of approximately 500 residents, this island city was officially incorporated in 1817, making it one of Michigan's oldest settlements. Nestled in the north-east part of the state, Mackinac Island falls within a unique region, bordered by Lake Huron and Lake Michigan. This city's classification is distinctly rural, as it is renowned for its absence of automobiles, relying instead on horse-drawn carriages and bicycles for transportation. The average income level of Mackinac Island, at $22,625 per year, is somewhat lower than the rest of the state, reflecting the island's reliance on tourism and seasonal employment.
