Flint, a city in Michigan, stands as a distinctive urban center in the state's landscape. With a population of approximately 95,000 residents, Flint was officially incorporated in 1855, making it one of Michigan's older cities. Located in the southeastern part of the state, Flint is classified as urban, with a historically strong presence in the automotive and manufacturing industries. However, the city has faced economic challenges in recent years, resulting in an average income level of approximately $26,000 per year, significantly lower than the state average. This income disparity reflects the city's struggles with deindustrialization and the ongoing need for revitalization efforts to address its economic and social challenges.
